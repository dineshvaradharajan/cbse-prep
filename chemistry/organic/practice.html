Q1
SN2 reaction proceeds with:
Correct: C ‚Äî Walden inversion. In SN2, the nucleophile attacks from the back (anti to the leaving group) in a single concerted step. This causes the spatial arrangement to invert ‚Äî like an umbrella flipping. R configuration gives S, and vice versa. Complete inversion (100%) distinguishes SN2 from SN1 (racemisation).

Correct: D ‚Äî (CH‚ÇÉ)‚ÇÉCBr (tert-butyl bromide). SN1 proceeds via a carbocation intermediate. Tertiary carbocations are most stable (hyperconjugation + inductive effect of 3 alkyl groups). SN1 reactivity: 3¬∞ > 2¬∞ > 1¬∞ > CH‚ÇÉ. CH‚ÇÉBr is most reactive for SN2.
Q3

Correct: B ‚Äî CH‚ÇÉX > 1¬∞ > 2¬∞ > 3¬∞. SN2 requires backside attack. Steric hindrance increases from CH‚ÇÉ to 3¬∞. CH‚ÇÉX has no substituents on carbon ‚Üí least hindered ‚Üí fastest SN2. 3¬∞ has three bulky groups blocking nucleophile approach ‚Üí slowest SN2.
Q4
Which of the following is least reactive towards nucleophilic substitution?
Correct: C ‚Äî Chlorobenzene. The C‚ÄìCl bond in aryl halides has partial double bond character due to resonance (lone pair of Cl delocalised into the ring). This makes the C‚ÄìCl bond stronger and shorter ‚Üí much harder to break. Also, carbon is sp¬≤ hybridised (less available for SN2 backside attack).
Q5
In Finkelstein reaction, alkyl iodide is prepared from:
Correct: A. Finkelstein reaction: R‚ÄìCl + NaI ‚Üí R‚ÄìI + NaCl (in dry acetone). NaCl precipitates out (insoluble in acetone), driving the equilibrium forward. Similarly R‚ÄìBr + NaI ‚Üí R‚ÄìI + NaBr. This is an SN2 reaction.
Q6
Which of the following solvents favours SN1 over SN2?
Correct: B ‚Äî Water (polar protic). SN1 produces a carbocation intermediate that needs solvation (stabilisation) by polar protic solvents (water, alcohols) which can form H-bonds. Polar aprotic solvents (DMSO, acetone, DMF) favour SN2 by not solvating (and thereby not slowing down) the nucleophile.

Correct: C. In sucrose, the glycosidic linkage uses C-1 of Œ±-glucose and C-2 of Œ≤-fructose (both anomeric positions).
